# Healdton Service Center — Project Idea

## Client / Shop Details

- **Shop name:** Healdton Service Center
- **Owner:** Logan
- **Primary user:** Logan and shop staff
- **Initial goal:** Build a simple internal shop management app for Healdton Service Center that covers Logan's daily workflow without the cost and complexity of ShopMonkey.

## Logan's Confirmed Requirements

Logan said the app needs to track:
- Inventory, including part numbers, pricing, and quantities
- Sales reports
- Customer information, including vehicle information and maintenance logs
- Invoices
- Payments received against invoices

## The Problem

Logan is opening/running Healdton Service Center and was quoted:
- **$10,000 upfront** to onboard with ShopMonkey
- **$150/month** ongoing to continue using it

That's a steep barrier for a small shop owner just getting started.

## The Opportunity

Build a competitor to ShopMonkey targeted at independent tire and auto shops — simpler, cheaper, and built for the small business owner.

## Why We Can Win

- ShopMonkey's pricing is enterprise-heavy and not small-shop friendly
- A leaner product focused on the core workflow (appointments, invoices, inventory, customer records) could undercut them significantly
- There's a large market of independent shops that are either unserved or overpaying

## Beta Testing Plan

Logan, the owner of Healdton Service Center, has agreed to be our first beta tester:
- If we build a competitive app, he'll use it **for free** during the testing phase
- Once stable, he'd pay **$75/month** for maintenance and support
- This gives us a real-world environment to test the app in an actual shop

This is a win-win — Logan saves thousands compared to ShopMonkey, and we get live feedback from a real user.

## Next Steps

- Build the login screen
- Build the internal MVP around Logan's confirmed requirements
- Continue talking to Logan about his day-to-day workflow and pain points
- Estimate what we could charge (e.g. $30–50/month) to be competitive
